I hate repeating myself! Start in Kenya, investigate crime scenes, and talk to the local police. See if you can find clues and track the suspect.

In a nutshell, your job is to discover the identity of NCS members and track them down within 100 hours. The apps we’ve installed on your smartphone will be crucially important in this endeavor.

1. The Phone app will help you obtain warrants for your arrests. Make you have a warrant before you stumble upon a criminal. Otherwise, we’ll have to release the goon.

2. The Criminal Database app contains the current information of all people suspected to belong to the NCS. You can filter out innocent suspects as you accumulate clues.

3. The Notepad app will keep track of any clues you find.

4. Through the jetBlue app you can travel all over the world. Each flight takes 10 hours.

5. If you’re bored, check out some videos on the youtube app.

6. The Investigate! app allows you to search crime scenes and talk to witnesses. Each action takes 2 hours, so make sure you don’t waste time investigating the same place twice.

7. The Wikipedia app is great for research. You might need help with a history question or two.

8. Everyone gets lost now and then. The Google Maps app will help you will geography puzzles.

9. As you travel around the world, you’re bound to meet people that don't speak English. Use the translate app to help you communicate.

10. Humanity is nothing without art. Use the Music app to listen to tunes while you sleuth.

Nice try detective, but it'll take more than a phone call to catch one of us!
